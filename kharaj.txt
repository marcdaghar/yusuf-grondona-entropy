Kharaj Tax Base, the number of establishments scales linearly with population:

text
Nf = η * N
where η ≈ 21.6 (people per establishment)
If Kharaj is a tax on land and commercial establishments (not income), the tax base is proportional to Nf. Therefore:

text
Kharaj Revenue = τ * Nf = τ * η * N
where τ is the Kharaj rate
Traditional Kharaj rates in Islamic history: 10-20% of produce from land (Umar ibn al-Khattab). For urban establishments, a comparable rate might be 5-10% of gross revenue or a fixed amount per establishment.

Total government size (Kharaj-funded) = weighted average of these functions:

text
Government_Size(N) = N^β_avg
where β_avg ≈ 0.92 (sub-linear)
This means: As cities grow, government becomes a smaller fraction of the economy (economies of scale). This is the opposite of modern welfare states, where government grows faster than population.
